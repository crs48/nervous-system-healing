# Content Guidelines

The voice and rules every page follows. Consolidated from explorations
0002–0007; this is the copy-review checklist of record.

## Voice

- **Warm, honest, of service.** Talk like a person who's walking the same
  road, not a brand or an authority. First person ("I") is welcome for the
  founder's own experience; keep it in service of the reader.
- **Literal and concrete.** Say "this takes 2 minutes, you'll look slowly
  around the room, there's no sound" — not "sink into your body's wisdom."
  Avoid idioms and metaphysics that a literal reader would trip on.
- **Invitational, with an exit.** Prompts offer, they don't command:
  "if it feels okay, you might…", "you can stop at any time." Every
  body-focused prompt names a way out.
- **Prompts that cause the noticing.** Prefer "there's a sound you'd stopped
  hearing — this sentence just sent your attention to it" over "notice the
  sounds around you." The sentence does the work.

## The slowness principle (0006)

- Short by default (2–5 minute practices). No urgency, no bingeing.
- End states, not next states: "That's a good place to stop. This will be
  here tomorrow." Never "keep your streak going."
- Progress is a spiral: setbacks can mean deeper material surfacing, not
  backsliding. Don't imply linear improvement.
- Name the efforting paradox where relevant: you can't force safety; slower
  usually is the fast way.
- **No streaks, scores, red zeros, "you missed N days", urgency, autoplay,
  sound-on-by-default, email capture, or infinite feeds.** Anywhere.

## Affirming & trauma-informed language (0002/0005)

Frame variation as difference, not deficit. Describe behavior by its cause.
Never write toward "normalizing" anyone.

| Avoid | Use |
|---|---|
| symptoms | traits |
| disorder / deficit / broken / wired wrong | difference / variation / doing its job |
| oversensitive / too sensitive | highly responsive; registers this more intensely |
| overreacting / dramatic | accurately reporting real input |
| fix / cure / normalize | support / accommodate / regulate |
| suffers from / afflicted with | has / experiences |

**Two standing hedges (honesty > affirmation):**

- **HSP / sensory sensitivity** — present as a spectrum trait, not a
  diagnosis.
- **Polyvagal / "your nervous system is protecting you"** — use the
  felt-sense reframe, but as a *map*, not settled biology ("a helpful way to
  think about it is…", never "science proves…").

Avoid inspiration-porn framing and any implied norm to measure against. The
message is *variation, not verdict.*

## Honesty (0003/0004)

- **Evidence and personal experience never merge in the same breath.**
  Evidence is graded (`EvidenceTag`) and cited; personal experience sits in a
  visually distinct `NOfOneAside` labelled "one person, not a study."
- Grade *claims*, not people. Report null/weak results honestly.
- Cite factual claims. Every modality and learn page carries a
  `lastReviewed` date.
- Clinical modalities (medication-assisted therapies especially) are
  refer-out only — no dosing, no sourcing, no efficacy overclaiming.

## Page anatomy (0007)

Every content page: breadcrumb → H1 → **lead that could be the whole page**
(never teases) → body that's complete without opening anything → optional
`GoDeeper` (one level) → the leaf motif → `NextSteps` (≤3 links: one deeper,
one sideways, one back) → warm footer sign-off.

## Design coherence (0007)

- Tokens only — no ad-hoc colors, fonts, radii, shadows. See `/style`.
- No pure `#fff` / `#000` anywhere.
- At most ~3 decorative motifs per view; a new motif retires an old one.
- Motion honors `prefers-reduced-motion`; nothing autoplays; whimsy only in
  rare states (404, end-of-article).
