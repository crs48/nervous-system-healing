Somatic Experiencing is a body-first approach to trauma developed by Peter
Levine. Instead of retelling what happened, you and the practitioner track
body sensation in small, careful doses — the working idea is that survival
energy that never got to complete (the fight that couldn't happen, the run
that never ran) stays held in the body, and can finish in slow motion, drop
by drop.

Two words you'll hear constantly: **titration** (working with the smallest
possible amount of activation at a time) and **pendulation** (moving between
that small activation and a felt sense of okayness, rather than staying in
the hard stuff). The pacing isn't a limitation of the method — it *is* the
method.

Sessions are usually seated conversation with a lot of "what do you notice
now?" — some practitioners also use supportive touch (with consent,
clothed). It tends to be slow work over months, not weeks.
