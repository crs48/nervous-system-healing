Internal Family Systems, developed by Richard Schwartz, works from the idea
that the mind is naturally made of "parts" — and that underneath them all is
a calm, curious core it calls the Self. The parts aren't problems to remove;
they're seen as protectors doing their best, often stuck in the role they took
on during a hard time.

In practice, you turn toward a part with curiosity instead of judgment — the
anxious planner, the harsh critic, the one that numbs out — and get to know
what it's afraid would happen if it stopped. A lot of people find it a
gentler, less shaming way to meet the stuff they'd usually fight with
themselves about.

It's mostly talk-based, though it pairs naturally with body awareness.
