Feldenkrais is a method of movement education — slow, gentle, exploratory
sequences that invite you to notice how you move and discover easier
alternatives, rather than being told the "right" way. It grew out of Moshe
Feldenkrais's work and is often done lying on the floor, following spoken
prompts through small, unhurried movements.

For people whose bodies have been braced and guarded for a long time — a
common thread in nervous-system dysregulation — the appeal is the pace and the
lack of pressure. Nothing is corrected; you're just given room to feel what
you're doing and let it reorganize. It sits in this directory as supportive
movement, not a trauma therapy.
