Tai chi and qigong are slow, flowing movement practices that pair gentle
motion with breath and attention. They come from Chinese tradition and are
practiced by millions of people daily, often outdoors in groups.

For nervous-system purposes, the useful thing is that they're a way to be
present and regulated *while moving* — which suits a lot of people far better
than sitting still and watching the breath. The evidence for mood is genuinely
decent, roughly on par with other exercise, and the barrier to entry is about
as low as it gets. Think of it as accessible, regulating movement you can do
almost anywhere.
