EMDR is the most strongly evidenced approach in this directory for
post-traumatic stress. You bring to mind a distressing memory while the
therapist guides sets of side-to-side eye movements (or taps, or tones) — a
process thought to help the brain reprocess a memory that got "stuck" in a raw,
unfiled state, so it can settle into the past where it belongs.

It's more structured than open-ended talk therapy, and often works over a
defined number of sessions rather than indefinitely. Because it deliberately
approaches charged material, a well-trained EMDR therapist spends real time on
safety and resourcing first — which is exactly the pacing this whole site
keeps coming back to.
