Bodywork covers a range of hands-on approaches — deep-tissue massage, Rolfing,
structural integration, myofascial release — that work with the body's soft
tissue and fascia, often alongside movement.

This entry is a good example of why this directory grades things honestly. The
*specific stories* these methods sometimes tell — that fascia holds trauma and
can be manually released — run well ahead of the evidence. And yet many people
(including me — see the note above) find real relief, ease, and settling from
skilled, attuned touch. Part of that is almost certainly the nervous-system
effect of safe co-regulation with another person, which is genuinely powerful.

So: worth trying if it helps you, with clear eyes about what can and can't be
claimed. Skilled touch that leaves you calmer is a real thing; the tissue
mechanism is where the honesty caveat lives.
