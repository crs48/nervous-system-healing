  5-MeO-DMT is unusual among psychedelics: it acts mostly on the calming
  5-HT1A receptor rather than the 5-HT2A receptor that drives the visual,

  distinct, largely non-visual, all-encompassing "held by the universe"

  ceremonial use. It is also the highest-risk item on this page: extremely
  potent and fast, with real cardiovascular load, and deaths have been reported
  — especially when combined with MAOI antidepressants (serotonin syndrome) or
  taken as toad-derived material that also contains heart-active toxins.

5-MeO-DMT produces, for many people, a fast and total dissolving of the
ordinary self into something that feels boundless and safe. Its receptor
profile — calming 5-HT1A rather than the more activating 5-HT2A — helps explain
why it can feel so different from LSD or mushrooms.

Held honestly, this belongs in the "borrowed state" column, and it's the door
that most needs its risks named alongside its beauty. The subjective calm is
real; the compound is still acutely activating and genuinely dangerous without
screening, a sober setting, and support. There is no how-to here on purpose.
What a glimpse like this is *good* for is orientation: it can show you the room
exists. Learning to find your way back to it — slowly, safely, on your own — is
the work this site is actually about.
