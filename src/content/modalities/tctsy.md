Trauma-sensitive yoga takes ordinary gentle yoga and strips out everything
coercive about it. There are no adjustments, no "hold it here," no mirror, no
performance. The whole point is the opposite: you're constantly invited to
notice what you feel and to choose — bend more or less, or not at all.

That emphasis on choice and interoception (sensing your own body from the
inside) is what makes it trauma-sensitive, and it's why the studied form is a
specific credential rather than just "yoga for stress." For people for whom
sitting still with the mind is too much, moving the body with this much
permission can be a kinder doorway.
