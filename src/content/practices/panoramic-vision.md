  invite the calmer, more open state.

**This takes about 2 minutes. You'll soften your gaze to take in the edges of
your vision. No sound, eyes open.**

1. Pick a comfortable spot in front of you to rest your eyes on — don't stare,
   just let them settle there.
2. Now, without moving your eyes, let your attention widen to what's at the
   edges — above, below, out to both sides. The room is bigger than the point
   you're looking at.
3. Let your focus stay soft and panoramic, taking in the whole scene at once
   rather than any one thing. Many people find their breath drops or their
   shoulders ease when they do this.
4. Stay with the wide view for a few slow breaths.
5. When you're ready, let your eyes go back to normal, and notice any
   difference.

If widening your vision feels strange or unpleasant, just let your eyes do
what they want — there's no wrong way. Stop any time.
