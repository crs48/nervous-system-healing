**This takes about 2 minutes. You'll look for one small good-feeling thing
and stay with it a moment. No sound, any position.**

1. Glance around, out a window, or into your own memory of today, for
   something that gives even the faintest positive flicker — warm light, a
   plant, a pet, a color you like, a face you're fond of, a decent cup of
   something.
2. When you land on one, stay with it a few seconds longer than you normally
   would. Let yourself actually take it in.
3. See if you can notice where that little "hm, nice" registers in your body —
   a small softening in the chest, the shoulders, the jaw. If you can't feel
   anything, that's completely fine; the noticing is enough.
4. That's the whole practice. One glimmer, savored a little. You can do this
   any time, in seconds.

There's no need to manufacture a big feeling. Small and real beats big and
forced — that's true of almost everything here.
