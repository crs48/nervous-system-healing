**This takes about 3 minutes. You'll press against a solid wall with real
effort, then rest. No sound needed. Skip it if pushing hard isn't right for
your body today.**

1. Stand facing a solid wall, arm's length away, palms flat on it about
   shoulder height.
2. Press into the wall — a firm, steady push, like you're trying to move it.
   Let your legs and back join in. Somewhere around half to three-quarters of
   your effort, not a maximal strain. Keep breathing.
3. Hold the push for a slow count of about five to ten, feeling the work in
   your arms, shoulders, legs.
4. Then let go all at once and stand easy. Let your arms hang. Notice
   whatever's there — tingling, warmth, a shakiness, a breath that wants to
   come. That "after" is where the settling happens.
5. Repeat two or three times if it feels good, resting fully between.

Go easy if you have any shoulder, wrist, or heart concerns — this is meant to
feel like satisfying effort, never a strain. You can stop whenever.
