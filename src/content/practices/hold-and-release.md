whyItWorks: >
  Holding a breath while you tense your whole body raises the pressure inside
  your chest for a few seconds. When you let go and breathe out long, your body
  answers with a settling reflex — the pressure drops, and a calming, vagal
  "rest" signal comes through. Tensing first (the way progressive muscle
  relaxation does) makes the release easier to feel, and the long exhale adds
  to it. Together it's a small, deliberate version of the wave of settling that
  can come after effort lets go.

**This takes about 2 minutes. Sitting down, you'll take a breath, tense your
whole body for a few seconds, then let it all go with a long exhale. No sound.
You can stop any time.**

Please skip this one — or check with a clinician first — if you have heart
disease, high or uncontrolled blood pressure, glaucoma or other eye or retinal
problems, or you're pregnant. The brief hold-and-tense raises the pressure in
your chest and eyes for a moment, which isn't right for everyone. Do it seated,
never in water or anywhere a few seconds of light-headedness wouldn't be safe.
This should feel like satisfying effort, never a hard strain.

1. Sit comfortably. Take an easy breath in — maybe three-quarters full, not a
   huge gulp — and gently hold it.
2. While you hold, tense your body: hands, arms, shoulders, belly, legs, and —
   if it feels okay — a firm squeeze around your ribs and chest, like you're
   bracing. Somewhere around half to three-quarters of your effort. Just for a
   slow count of about five. Keep it comfortable; don't strain against a
   locked throat.
3. Then let everything go at once — release the tension and let a long, slow
   breath fall out of you. Let your shoulders drop. Let the exhale run longer
   than feels necessary.
4. Sit easy and notice the "after": a wave of heaviness or warmth, a slowing
   down, a breath that wants to come on its own. That settling is the whole
   point.
5. If it felt good, do it once or twice more, resting fully in between. Two or
   three rounds is plenty.

If the hold feels uncomfortable at any point, just stop and breathe normally —
or try [a longer exhale](../long-exhale/) on its own, which reaches the same
settling more gently, with none of the pressure.
