**This takes about 2 minutes. You'll hum quietly. It makes gentle sound, so
you might want a bit of privacy — but even a barely-there hum works.**

1. Take an easy breath in — nothing big.
2. On the way out, let a soft "mmmm" hum come. Any pitch, whatever's
   comfortable. Feel for a faint buzzing in your lips, face, throat, or chest.
3. Let the hum ride the whole exhale, then breathe in and go again.
4. Play with it if you like — a little higher, a little lower, hum a
   half-familiar tune. See if any pitch buzzes more, or feels nicer.
5. Five or six hums is plenty. Notice how your throat, face, or chest feel
   afterward.

If sound isn't possible or welcome right now, a long quiet sigh on the
out-breath is a gentler cousin of the same thing.
